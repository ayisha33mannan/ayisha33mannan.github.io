History

Al AinZoo

Al Ain Zoo, located in the city of Al Ain in the United Arab Emirates, is one of the region's premier wildlife parks. It was established in 1968 by the late Sheikh Zayed bin Sultan Al Nahyan, the founding father of the UAE, who had a profound passion for nature and conservation. The zoo was created to provide a safe haven for endangered species and to promote conservation awareness among the public.


Early Years and Development

In its early years, Al Ain Zoo started with a modest collection of animals, focusing primarily on species native to the Arabian Peninsula. The zoo quickly became a popular attraction for both residents and visitors, offering a unique opportunity to experience the region's wildlife up close.


Expansion and Modernization

Over the decades, Al Ain Zoo underwent significant expansions and modernization efforts. The zoo introduced various international species and developed state-of-the-art facilities to enhance the care and welfare of its animals. The creation of naturalistic habitats, such as the African Savannah exhibit and the Big Cat House, provided more enriching environments for the animals and a more immersive experience for visitors.


Today and Beyond

Today, Al Ain Zoo is home to over 4,000 animals, including many endangered species. It remains a beloved destination for families, tourists, and wildlife enthusiasts. As it looks to the future, Al Ain Zoo continues to prioritize animal welfare, conservation, and education, ensuring that it remains a beacon of wildlife preservation in the region.

Key Dates from Our History:

Know more about us!!

1968

Al Ain Zoo is founded by the late Sheikh Zayed bin Sultan Al Nahyan, the visionary leader of the United Arab Emirates, with a mission to promote wildlife conservation and environmental education.

1971

Al Ain Zoo officially opens its doors to the public, offering visitors a unique opportunity to experience the region's diverse wildlife and natural habitats.

1982

The Sheikh Zayed Desert Learning Centre is established within Al Ain Zoo, providing educational exhibits and resources about the UAE's desert environment and biodiversity.

1998

Al Ain Zoo initiates its first captive breeding program for endangered Arabian species, including the Arabian oryx and sand gazelle, to support their conservation and reintroduction into the wild.

2008

Al Ain Zoo introduces interactive educational programs and experiences, including guided tours, animal encounters, and workshops, to engage visitors of all ages in conservation and environmental stewardship.

2013

Al Ain Zoo celebrates its 45th anniversary with the opening of new exhibits and attractions, including the Sheikh Zayed Children's Garden and the Conservation Carousel, further enhancing the visitor experience.

2017

Al Ain Zoo launches its "Conservation Matters" initiative, partnering with local and international organizations to address pressing conservation issues and protect endangered species both within and beyond the zoo's borders.

2020

Al Ain Zoo announces its ambitious "Vision 2030" strategic plan, outlining its commitment to advancing wildlife conservation, sustainability, and education over the next decade.

2022

Al Ain Zoo expands its conservation efforts by launching several new initiatives, including the establishment of a dedicated research and breeding center for endangered Arabian species and the implementation of community-based conservation projects in collaboration with local communities.

2024

Al Ain Zoo commemorates its 56th anniversary with the introduction of innovative conservation programs and immersive visitor experiences, reaffirming its commitment to preserving biodiversity and inspiring future generations to become stewards of the natural world.
